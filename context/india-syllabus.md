## 📐 Topic 1: Geometry (Basic Geometrical Ideas)

### Class 6 (Beginner)
| Sub-topic | NCERT Chapter | Key Concepts |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| Bindu (Point) | Ch 4: Basic Geometrical Ideas | Position, no dimension, label with capital letter |
| Rekha (Line) | Ch 4 | Straight, infinite, arrowheads, named by 2 points |
| Khand (Segment) | Ch 4 | Part of line, 2 endpoints, measurable |
| Kiran (Ray) | Ch 4 | One endpoint, extends infinitely one direction |
| Shikhar (Vertex/Angle) | Ch 4 | Meeting point of 2 rays, angle arms |

**India Examples:**
- Points on a Delhi Metro map (stations as points)
- Lines like Rajpath stretching straight
- Segments like Delhi-Meerut Expressway section
- Rays like sunlight through a window
- Angles in a kite (patang ke kone), roof slopes

### Class 7 (Intermediate)
| Sub-topic | NCERT Chapter | Key Concepts |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| Lines & Angles | Ch 5 | Parallel, perpendicular, transversal |
| Triangles | Ch 6 | Types by sides and angles, angle sum 180° |
| Congruence | Ch 7 | Same shape and size, SSS, SAS, ASA |
| Practical Geometry | Ch 10 | Construction with compass/ruler |

**India Examples:**
- Parallel: Railway tracks (Indian Railways)
- Transversal: Ladder placed on ground
- Triangles: Trikon shape in rangoli patterns
- Congruence: Same-sized gulab jamuns at Haldiram

### Class 8 (Expert)
| Sub-topic | NCERT Chapter | Key Concepts |

## 🏫 School Context Notes

- **Curriculum:** CBSE / NCERT (pan-India, most widely followed)
- **Medium:** Hinglish (Hindi + English blend) — students are comfortable with mixed-language math instruction
- **Student profile:** Ages 11-14, Delhi NCR region reference, urban and semi-urban
- **Math anxiety:** High at Class 6-7 transition; need encouragement, street-smart analogies
- **Computation tools:** No calculators in Class 6; basic allowed in Class 8
